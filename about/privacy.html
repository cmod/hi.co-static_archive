Welcome to Hi.

This privacy policy (“Policy”) describes how Moments Management Corp. and its related companies (“Company”) collect, use and share personal information of users of this website, http://hi.co (the “Site”) and Services (as defined in the Terms of Use (where such term includes, without limitation, the Site and mobile applications)). Our Services provide you with a platform to access, share, and publish stories and experiences from places around the world in various formats subject to the Terms of Use and this Policy. You understand and agree that your User Content is made public, and may be accessed and viewed by other registered and unregistered users of the Services. This means that other Users may search for, see, use, or share any of your User Content that you make publicly available through the Services, consistent with the terms and conditions of this Privacy Policy and our Terms of Use.

INFORMATION WE COLLECT

We collect the following types of information:

  • Information We Get From You. If you wish to have access to certain functionalities of our Services you will be required to become a registered user and provide certain information about yourself. You may also decide to post and provide content and information through our Services. Youu may, however, visit our Site without providing this information. We collect your name, email address, phone number, picture, username, password, biographical information (including data in your biographical information such as demographic information (for example, occupation), professional title and company name), your location, information contained in content that you upload (e.g., photos, stories, comments and other materials) and feedback you provide, and other information you directly give us through our Services.
  • Information We Get from Others. We may get information about you from other sources. We may add this to information we get through our Services.
  • Information Automatically Collected. We automatically log information about you and your computer or mobile device. For example, when you visit or use our Services, we log your IP address, browser type and the domain from which you are visiting, operating system, platform, the search terms you used to find the Services or search terms used on the Services, any advertisements on which you click, your country, city and general location as determined by your IP address, GPS coordinates (if you enable this feature), a device identifier (but not the UDID), information about your use of and actions on our Services. You can enable or disable location services when you use our Services at any time, through your mobile device settings.
  • Cookies. We log information using “cookies.” Cookies are small data files stored on your hard drive by a website. Cookies help us make our Site and your visit better. We use cookies to manage registered user sessions, see which parts of our Site people use, and to count visits to our Site.
  • Web Beacons. We or our partners may log information using digital images called Web beacons or Clear GIFs on our Site or in our emails.Web beacons are used to manage cookies, count visits, and to learn what marketing works and what does not. Web Beacons can also be used to tell if you open or act on our emails.
  • Metadata. Metadata is usually technical data that is associated with user content. For example, Metadata can describe how, when and by whom a piece of user content was collected and how that content is formatted. Users can add or may have Metadata added to their User Content including tags or topic markers (e.g., to provide general context for your moment), “thanks” or “tell me more” actions, local weather information, comments or other data. This makes your User Content more searchable by others and more interactive.
  • Information Collected from Social Networking Sites or Other Third Party Companies. If you log on to the Services with your login credentials from a social networking site (e.g., facebook), we may receive information about you from such social networking site, in accordance with the terms of use and privacy policy of the third party provider of such social networking site. For example, facebook may share your friend list with us, in order to help us establish your account and find other Services users that you know. You understand that by granting us access to your social networking service account as part of the functionality of the Services, we will have access to your content, including personally identifiable information, at such social networking service account, and we may access, make available, use and store such content so it is available via your Services account. You may disable the connection by accessing Facebook’s third party application settings or emailing us at help@hi.co. However, as noted above, your relationship with the social networking service provider, their collection, use, disclosure and storage of information, security and privacy policies and practices are governed solely by the policies of the social networking services, and we will have no liability or responsibility for the privacy practices or other actions of any social networking service that may be enabled within the Services. We may also receive information about you from companies that provide services, such as Google Analytics, to us. We may add this information to the information we have already collected from you via the Service.

Miscellany
  • The name Hi comes from the word Hitotoki, which was the name of the online publication from which this product draws inspiration. Hitotoki is a Japanese noun comprised of two components: hito or “one” and toki or “time,” and is often translated as “a moment.” In common usage, it can be used to describe any brief, singular stretch of time (if we share a meal someday, you can call that a hitotoki).
